About the Studio

This is an opportunity for anyone and everyone who has an interest in learning and doing all things DJ. At heart, DJing is an evolving and collective art form, stretched somewhere between curator, musician, engineer and social facilitator. It's an awkward space, with a lack of IRL support, mentorship, resource sharing and collaboration. The goal of this workshop is to start a momentum to make this space happen. In this introductory studio you'll meet with an exceptionally well-rounded mentor, discuss a DJ paradigm that frames modern DJing in an actionable and productive perspective, and have a chance to try out different kinds of gear. If this is your idea of a good time, sign up and save the date. It's about time you found a space that speaks your language.

Hosted by


Leila Paper is a DJ emeritus that moved into the industry as a broadcast engineer and radio DJ in 1991 and never left the DJ world. Boasting a parent-disappointing depth of practical and theoretical knowledge and experience a long career ranging from raves, clubs, festivals and hospitality, she is an ideal mentor and advocate. She will share her experience engineering perspective of sound systems and musicianship, understand DJ hardware so that you can DIY and still sound professional, and share strategies DJs use to navigate the shifting crate digging landscape. Do bring all your questions. This is the space to get answers in the spirit of support and non-judgemental encouragement.
